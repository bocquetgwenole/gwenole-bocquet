Framework

The central claim of this research program is that classical spacetime geometry should not be taken as a primitive microscopic arena for open quantum sources. The native level of the theory is instead formulated on an effective full-rank manifold of density operators, equipped with a GKSL open-system dynamics and, in the detailed-balance sector, with a quantum optimal-transport geometry, entropy-gradient-flow structure, and native entropic ordering. In this sense, the primitive ontology is a state-space OT/GKSL dynamics rather than a fundamental spacetime metric.

Classical geometry enters only at a higher certified layer. A geometric readout is admitted on a certified operational window Wacc, where stable record functionals define a local operational coframe and a reconstructed readout metric. The passage from native state-space dynamics to readable geometry is mediated by a local OT-to-readout bridge with controlled interface defects. This bridge is not an optional interpretive device: it is the structural mechanism by which native state-space information becomes geometrically readable on an admissible domain.

A central architectural constraint of the framework is the Einstein lock. The two-derivative Einstein-Hilbert kinetic block is kept strictly standard, with fixed universal G0. Readable state dependence is therefore excluded from the kinetic gravitational sector and displaced into a source-side constitutive channel together with the response/exchange structure required by covariant closure. This point sharply distinguishes the framework from generic low-energy modified-gravity deformations.

The corpus is organized as a hierarchy of logically distinct layers. First comes the native OT/GKSL dynamics on effective state space. On certified collective sectors, this admits exact reduced nonlinear equations; these are exact within the certified reduced description and are not weak-field approximations. Above that level, the reconstructed readout metric sector admits a certified nonlinear Einstein-locked closure. Only after these native, reduced, and readout structures are fixed do standard Dirac-, Maxwell-, Yang-Mills-, Poisson-, Newtonian-, and Einstein-like equations appear as controlled recoveries under additional assumptions such as weak field, quasi-staticity, symmetry reduction, and adiabaticity.

An important consequence is that the Newtonian sector is not the defining gravitational regime of the theory. It is a corollary of a more general nonlinear Einstein-locked readout closure. Likewise, temporal readability is not primitive. The framework distinguishes native entropic ordering, operational tick extraction, and certified temporal readout. The latter is admitted only on a stricter certified subwindow Wtick ⊂ Wacc, reflecting the fact that readable time is itself a finite-resource certified layer.

The practical motivation of the program is low-energy testability. The current experimental branch targets the visible source-side constitutive channel through calibrated state proxies and narrow-band lock-in observables. This means that the framework is not presented as a speculative ultraviolet completion, but as a closed certified-domain state-to-readout theory with an explicit route toward falsifiable low-energy tests.
