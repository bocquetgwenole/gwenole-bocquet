Framework

At native level, the theory is formulated on a finite effective manifold of faithful density operators governed by GKSL open-system dynamics. In the detailed-balance sector, this native dynamics carries a quantum optimal-transport geometry, entropy-gradient-flow structure, and a distinguished entropic ordering. The primitive arena is therefore not a spacetime metric but a state-space OT/GKSL dynamics on finite effective support.

Classical spacetime is admitted only as a certified readout layer. It is reconstructed through an operational map and a controlled OT-to-readout bridge, not postulated as a primitive microscopic background. The relevant question is therefore not whether geometry is “fundamental”, but under what precise conditions geometric, temporal, spacetime, and causal-local semantics are licensed by the certified readout architecture.

The geometric readout layer exists only on a certified access window Wacc, where stable record functionals define a readable operational coframe, rank conditions remain nondegenerate, and bridge defects remain controlled. Certified time is stricter: readable ticks exist only on a subwindow Wtick ⊂ Wacc. Full spacetime readability occupies a jointly certified window Wst ⊂ Wacc, where temporal, geometric, and bridge admissibility remain simultaneously compatible. Locality, causality, and relativity are then treated as still stricter certified readout properties, not as primitive microscopic axioms.

A central structural constraint is the Einstein lock. The two-derivative Einstein-Hilbert kinetic block is held in strictly standard form, with universal constant G0 and no readable state-dependent prefactor multiplying curvature. Readable preparation dependence is therefore forbidden in the kinetic gravitational sector and forced into a source-side constitutive channel together with the response/exchange structure required by covariant closure. This is a hard architectural lock, not a phenomenological convenience.

The framework is organized into logically distinct layers. At native level: OT/GKSL state-space dynamics. On certified reduced sectors: exact nonlinear reduced equations, not weak-field ansätze. On the readout side: a nonlinear Einstein-locked closure of the reconstructed metric block before any Newtonian or weak-field limit is taken. Standard Dirac-, Maxwell-, Yang-Mills-, Poisson-, Newtonian-, and Einstein-like equations appear only as controlled recoveries under additional hypotheses. They do not define the theory.

The framework also establishes theorem-level finite-resource boundaries of readout. Temporal readability, geometric readability, spacetime readability, and causal-local semantics are all finite-budget structures on finite effective support. The cutoff is therefore not a disposable regulator but a structural marker of finite spectral and informational support. What fails near the certified boundary is not necessarily the native dynamics, but the license to keep using the same classical spacetime language with the same certified status.

Beyond architecture, the theory already produces nontrivial source-side physics. In the exact reduced constitutive-holonomic sector, stable nontrivial branches can be selected internally by constitutive-holonomic balance. These branches generate a positive reduced effective mass scale, a generically nonzero vacuum-like residual energy, and a branch-resolved source-side structure. The vacuum-like residual lifts consistently into the Einstein-locked source/response closure without moving state dependence into the kinetic gravitational sector.

The same branch architecture supports more than one physical regime. The corpus now distinguishes: a visible mass-bearing branch; a vacuum-like branch whose residual contribution populates the effective source-side vacuum slot after closure; and an intermediate CDM-like branch that remains materially source-active while becoming dark in the ordinary visible readout channel through suppression of visibility transfer. In this framework, dark-matter-like behavior is treated as an internal branch property of source-side matter, not as a new primitive particle sector appended by hand.

A homogeneous closed specialization of the theory has also been constructed. In that sector, the readout scale factor, the homogeneous constitutive variable, the source/response closure, and the finite spacetime budget form a closed Einstein-locked dynamical system. Within this system, the vacuum-like slot associated with the a0 block acquires a non-ad hoc effective interpretation after source/response closure. In a flat quasi-de Sitter slow regime it contributes as an effective vacuum-like density; in a closed quasi-static regime it plays a compensating role together with positive curvature. The budget acts there as a branch-selection and branch-stabilization constraint, not as an added fluid.

The framework is therefore not merely conceptual. It contains exact reduced differential structures, certified readout geometry, nonlinear Einstein-locked closure, theorem-level temporal and spacetime solvability results, causal-local certification, reduced source-side mass generation, vacuum-like residual sourcing, a theorem on a CDM-like intermediate branch, and an explicit low-energy operational test branch based on calibrated state proxies, branch discrimination, and narrow-band lock-in observables.

Its main strength is not rhetorical breadth but structural discipline: fixed native ontology, explicit certification domains, finite-resource solvability, hard Einstein lock, exact reduced sectors, branch-resolved source-side outputs, and controlled readout lifting.
